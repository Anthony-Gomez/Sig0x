// The problem

Multisig is secure. Your signing process isn't.

M-of-N cryptography protects your vault. But the moment you're asked to sign, the attack surface opens wide.

[RED FLAG 01]
Address Poisoning
Attackers inject lookalike addresses in transaction history. One paste, one signature, and your funds are gone — multisig won't stop it.
[RED FLAG 02]
Blind Signing
When signers can't decode raw calldata, they sign what they're told. Smart contract payloads hide malicious logic in plain sight.
[RED FLAG 03]
Social Engineering
Urgent Slack messages. Spoofed coordinators. Fake governance votes. Human signers under pressure are the weakest link in any threshold scheme.
[RED FLAG 04]
Rogue Signer Collusion
When m signers are simultaneously compromised, no threshold saves you. Anomalous co-signing patterns go undetected without behavioral AI.
[RED FLAG 05]
Policy Drift
Spending limits. Whitelist bypasses. Over months, wallets accumulate policy exceptions that silently undermine the original security model.
[RED FLAG 06]
Cross-Chain Replay
Signatures valid on one chain are replayed on another. Multi-chain deployments face replay vectors that human review routinely misses.
